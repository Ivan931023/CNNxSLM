What the pipeline does

1. Background subtraction. Median of a 4 %-wide border ring, subtracted from the whole frame. Smoothing is used only to find the centre; the RMS metrics never see a smoothed pixel.

2. Centre detection. Otsu / 50 % threshold on a Gaussian-smoothed (σ=2 px) copy, weighted centroid of the largest connected component, then 3×3 parabolic sub-pixel refinement of the smoothed-intensity argmax.

3. Crop sizing. Four ways:

  • auto with reference: side = 500·√(Asrc/Aref) where A is the 5%-mask area. Preserves the simulation framing — the beam ends up occupying the same relative area of the 500×500 output as in the reference.
  • auto without reference: 2·max(W,H)5%-bbox·pad — beam fills the output. max(W,H), not the diagonal, so non-circular shapes fit.
  • radius: 2·Rexp·pad, Rexp from Calibration.html.
  • pixels: a fixed side directly.

4. Template registration. Refines (cx, cy) in source coordinates by phase-correlating the source patch against the theoretical reference resized to side × side source pixels. Uses the full intensity — captures the asymmetric lobes, ringing and dark-spot offsets of an aberrated flattop, which a contour-based aligner would miss. Sub-pixel via 3×3 parabolic refinement on the cross-correlation peak.

5. CNN input. Crop the source at the registered (cx, cy) with the chosen side; bicubic-resample to 500×500. This image is for the CNN only; the RMS metrics use the raw source pixels.

6. RMS2D on RAW source. σ of the raw source intensities over pixels whose value is ≥ 95 % of Isrc,max. No smoothing, no rescaling. Reported absolute (peak-normalised) and relative (σ/μ).

7. RMSall on RAW source. For each source pixel inside the crop region, the corresponding sub-pixel theoretical value is taken via the inverse crop transform (sim_x,sim_y) = (250 + (x−cx)·500/side, 250 + (y−cy)·500/side) and a bicubic sample on the 500×500 reference. RMS of (Itheo − Isrc) is then computed over the intersection of the two 5 % support masks in source coordinates, both peak-normalised. The experimental side of the difference is always a raw source pixel value.
