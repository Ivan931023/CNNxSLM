How the three stages work

Stage 1 — Coarse knife-edge. A half-plane phase grating x > sx sweeps across the SLM. The +1-order CCD intensity ∝ ∫|E|²·M dA — the integral of the beam under the masked region. Differentiating that transition curve yields the 1D projection of |E|², and its first moment is the centroid. Robust for any beam shape; unbiased; noise averages out.

Stage 2 — Fine sub-pixel scan. A small disk grating of radius R_spot is parked at offsets around the coarse centre. Its +1-order intensity is the beam profile convolved with the disk, peaking at the true centre. We fit the resulting 2D map (parabolic + weighted-centroid fallback) to recover the centre to ≪ 1 px.

Stage 3 — 1/e² beam size. Now centred on the fine estimate, sweep the radius R of a concentric disk grating. The +1-order intensity follows I(R) = A · ( 1 − exp(−2 R² / w²) ). Linear regression of ln(1 − I/A) vs R² seeds a non-linear fit that returns the 1/e² intensity radius w.

Physics shortcut used in this simulator

For a binary 0/π Ronchi grating gated by a 0/1 mask M(x,y), the +1 diffraction-order intensity equals |c+1|²·∫|Ein|²·M dA. We use that closed form instead of an FFT per scan step — algorithmically identical up to a constant. The full 1920×1080 FFT version is in src/Calibration_Simulation.py for diagnostic plots.
